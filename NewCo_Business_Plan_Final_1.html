Section 01

Executive Summary

NewCo is a boutique search firm specializing in junior talent placement across commercial real estate — including asset management, acquisitions, development, and credit roles. Founded in partnership with September Search, NewCo operates as the junior counterpart to September Search's established senior executive search practice, creating a comprehensive, end-to-end talent solution for real estate clients.

The firm is designed to fill a clear gap in the market: most boutique search firms focus exclusively on senior talent, leaving junior hiring — analysts, associates, and mid-level professionals — underserved. NewCo addresses this directly, with a dedicated approach that prioritizes candidate relationships, client engagement, and long-term talent pipeline development.

NewCo was founded in partnership with September Search, in an effort to provide our clients and candidates with greater optionality and visibility into the talent market. September Search is a new firm, but experienced team of search professionals dedicated to senior-level executive search, on a retained basis. NewCo will serve as the junior partner to September Search, working with young professionals across the commercial real estate landscape. For our candidates, this means that as you gain experience, you already have a team who understands your long-term goals standing by. For our clients, we will have dedicated teams getting to know talent at every level, so no matter the position or seniority, you have a centralized resource.

  • Specialization — deep expertise in CRE junior talent.

NewCo occupies a deliberate gap in the commercial real estate search market: the junior level. Section 03

Market Opportunity

The Junior CRE Talent Gap

Commercial real estate remains one of the most relationship-driven industries in the world — yet the infrastructure for finding and placing junior talent has not kept pace with the industry's growth. Most boutique search firms focus exclusively on VP-level and above, leaving a significant and recurring hiring need unaddressed at the analyst and associate tier.

Key Demand Drivers
  • PE real estate firms, debt funds, and developers hire 2–6 junior professionals per year on average.
  • Most do not have dedicated in-house recruiters at the junior level.
  • Campus recruiting is losing ground to experienced hire search as firms seek candidates with 1–3 years of experience.
  • The 2021–2023 hiring boom created a generation of junior CRE professionals now ready to make their first lateral move.
  • Real estate credit has expanded significantly, creating sustained demand for credit-trained junior talent.
Competitive Landscape

The majority of large, well-resourced organizations with sizable research and search teams operating at scale are utilized for junior search. While sometimes effective in the short term, this model often produces weak candidate relationships and long-term client attrition — the inevitable result of transaction-oriented hiring practices that prioritize volume over quality.

Smaller, boutique search firms have responded by focusing almost exclusively on senior-level mandates, where higher fees at lower volumes allow for the relationship depth and candidate quality their reputations demand. The junior market, by contrast, has been left largely unaddressed.

Until now, no firm has built a high-touch, specialized search practice dedicated exclusively to junior talent. We believe this is not only a viable model — it is an overdue one.

Junior professionals frequently make poor first or second career moves, decisions that can have lasting consequences on their long-term trajectory. Clients, in turn, routinely overspend on junior hiring as a direct result of high turnover driven by search partners who move quickly but not thoughtfully. NewCo is designed to address both problems. We aim to serve as trusted advisors — to junior talent navigating early career decisions, and to the teams who need a durable, long-term solution to junior hiring and the turnover costs that come with getting it wrong.

Our model does not require premium fees. By maintaining robust, continuously cultivated candidate pipelines, we are able to match prevailing market fee levels while delivering a meaningfully more specialized and considered search process.

For existing clients of September Search, this partnership introduces something new: a dedicated junior search resource that operates entirely outside the competition for your senior mandates. NewCo focuses exclusively on building out your junior team, while our partners at September Search remain focused on your highest-impact, senior-level opportunities — without conflict of interest.

By embedding ourselves exclusively in the junior talent landscape, we will earn the trust and long-term loyalty of candidates at the earliest stages of their careers. That depth of relationship makes us better partners for our clients, and a lasting, holistic resource for candidates as they grow.

Section 04

Service Model & Fee Structure

Exclusive Contingency Search

NewCo's default model with a 3-month exclusive timeline. Beneficial for onboarding new clients with less upfront cost as we build new relationships. A fee is collected only upon successful placement — no upfront cost to the client. After 3 months without placement, searches automatically shift to a retained structure.

Retained Search

Used for searches outside our core focus areas, or when a client specifically requires the retained model. A retainer — typically one-third of the total fee — is paid upfront, with the balance due on placement.

  • Guarantee does not apply in cases of layoff, role elimination, or client-initiated termination
  • Extended 6-month guarantee available as a premium offering for retained clients

Section 08

Search Process

A consistent, repeatable process is NewCo's core product. Every search follows the same seven-step methodology regardless of role type or client.

01
Client Intake & Role Specification
Deep intake call. Draft written role spec for client sign-off. Agree on compensation range, timeline, and interview process before any sourcing begins.
Day 1–3
02
Market Mapping
Identify 40–60 target candidates via LinkedIn Recruiter, ATS, and network. Prioritize employed candidates not actively looking.
Day 3–7
03
Outreach & Screening
Personalized outreach to all targets. Expect 15–20% response rate. 15–20 minute phone screen for all interested candidates before any client presentation.
Day 5–14
04
Candidate Presentation
Present 4–6 candidates with a written profile for each — background summary, motivation, comp expectations, and NewCo's assessment.
Day 14–30
05
Interview Coordination
Own the scheduling, candidate prep, and debrief calls. Contact both sides within 24 hours of every interview. Push for timely decisions.
Day 14–45
06
Offer Management
Secure verbal offer before written. Know the candidate's walk-away number, counter-offer likelihood, and competing situations. Close proactively.
Day 45–60
07
Post-Placement Follow-Up
Check in with client and candidate at 30, 60, and 90 days post-start. Document outcomes. Use to generate referrals and secure the next search.
30 / 60 / 90 days post-start
Section 09

Hiring & Team Building

Hiring Philosophy

NewCo will remain lean and founder-led through Year 1. Adding headcount before the business model is fully validated is a common early-stage mistake. The goal in Year 1 is to prove the process, build the client base, and generate enough revenue to justify and fund the first hire. Every hire should be a force multiplier — freeing the founder's time for higher-value client and candidate work.

The hiring strategy will remain adaptable to account for increased mandates or slower periods. Each team member will be expected to contribute across multiple dimensions — client interaction, candidate sourcing, and administrative support — with the mix evolving as the team grows.

Break-Even Analysis

At a monthly burn of approximately $10,884 and an average fee of $65,000 per placement, NewCo needs roughly 1 placement per quarter to cover operating costs. With 10–12 placements targeted in Year 1, the business has a meaningful cushion above break-even — assuming placements are distributed across the year.

Partnership Terms & Governance

The below represents a proposed term sheet for a new venture. This term sheet is non-binding and subject to definitive documentation.
Strategy
  • NewCo will focus on originating and executing recruitment for real estate clients, with a primary focus on junior-level roles (up to senior associate)
  • September Search will make introductions to NewCo for roles that fall outside the scope of September Search's work, as appropriate, and maintains right of first refusal for searches they originate
  • For VP-level and above roles originated by NewCo, NewCo will consult the partnership regarding the appropriate firm to execute the search
Corporate Structure & Economics
Ownership
  • 66.67% — September Search Partners LLC
  • 33.33% — Founder (Mollie Johnson)
  • Entity structured as an LLC
Founder Compensation
  • Salary: $100,000 per annum (pre-profit cost, not a draw)
  • Full benefits for individual or family
  • 401(k) per September Search's current scheme
  • Profit share: 33.33% after return of initial capital to SSP
Infrastructure & Investment
  • NewCo benefits from SSP infrastructure including accounting, payroll, bookkeeping, corporate tax preparation, legal counsel, office space, marketing, and LinkedIn Recruiter Pro
  • All infrastructure costs flow through NewCo's P&L at SSP group pricing
  • SSP will fund NewCo for startup costs and working capital, initially up to $125,000
  • Founder will dedicate 100% of working time to NewCo unless otherwise agreed
Profit Waterfall
  • Return of initial capital investment to SSP
  • Pro rata distribution: 33.33% to Founder, 66.67% to SSP
  • For the avoidance of doubt: the $100,000 salary is a pre-profit cost to the company. Profit share is in addition to salary.
Revenue Allocation
  • All revenues to NewCo shall flow through NewCo's P&L
  • NewCo will also earn revenue from SSP when a NewCo employee executes search work for SSP per written request
  • NewCo is entitled to 10% of the total fee for the first engagement with any client introduced to SSP that becomes fee-paying within 6 months of introduction
  • SSP is not entitled to direct referral fees from NewCo for searches originated by SSP and referred to NewCo — compensation is solely through profit share, unless both parties agree otherwise
Decision Rights
  • Business plan generated by Founder; reviewed and approved by majority vote of partners (Mollie Johnson, Emily Von Kohorn, Jennifer Novack)
  • Major decisions — hiring and firing, expenses above a defined threshold, distributions, and sale of the company — require majority vote of all partners
Section 14

Exit Strategy & Long-Term Vision

By the end of Year 3, NewCo will be at a natural inflection point — meaningful revenue, an established client base, a growing team, and a recognized brand. At that point, the partners will evaluate the path forward.

01
Remain Under the September Search Umbrella
Continue as a defined subsidiary or partner firm. Renegotiate terms to reflect NewCo's scale and value. Appropriate if the relationship is strong and brand association continues to generate value.
02
Go Fully Independent
Launch NewCo as a fully standalone firm. Exit the September Search partnership on agreed terms. Own 100% of revenue, brand, and client relationships. Appropriate if NewCo's pipeline is self-sustaining.
03
Merge or Affiliate More Formally
Explore deeper integration with September Search — a merger, equity exchange, or formal joint venture. Appropriate if a unified brand creates more value than two separate identities.
